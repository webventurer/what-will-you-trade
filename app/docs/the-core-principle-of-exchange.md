## Example 1: The cup of coffee

Why does a Starbucks latte cost $6 when coffee beans cost pennies? Because you're not paying for beans. You're paying for:

- A warm place to sit
- Free Wi-Fi
- A consistent experience anywhere in the world
- The identity of being a "Starbucks person"

That's value. Not in the beans. *In the context.*

---

## Example 2: The gym membership

Nobody pays $50 a month for access to machines. YouTube has free workouts. They pay for:

- Accountability
- Community
- The story they tell themselves: *"I'm someone who works on my health"*

The gym doesn't sell barbells. **It sells aspiration.**

## Example 3: The luxury watch

A Rolex doesn't tell time better than a $50 Timex. But it sells something different:

- Status
- Permanence
- The subtle language of wealth and success

The value isn't in the gears. **It's in the signal.**

## The exchange is always emotional

We pretend humans are rational actors, crunching numbers and calculating utility. We aren't.

We buy:

- Hope
- Relief
- Identity
- Belonging
- Power
- Comfort

**Money moves because of how something makes us feel.** And that feeling is the essence of perceived value.

---

## The law of asymmetry

Every transaction is an asymmetry:

- The buyer values what they're getting *more* than the money they're losing
- The seller values the money *more* than the thing they're selling

That asymmetry is where trade lives. That's why it works. **If both sides walk away feeling they won, that's sustainable value.**

If one side feels tricked? That's a scam. And scams always collapse.
